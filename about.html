About us

The Women’s Coaching Collective was established in 1993 by friends and business partners Valerie Emerson and Natalia Anne-Laure who saw a gap in the industry for female led coaching and mentorship. It all started around a kitchen table in Newcastle Upon Tyne but soon expended to offices across the Northeast.
By the year 2000 the collective had over 50 members across the North of England, each one an experienced business owner or executive who wanted to make a difference by passing on their knowledge to others.
Opening a London office in 2006, the collective chose to keep the head office in the heart of Newcastle to honour where it all started.
Holly Fungai was promoted to head of the collective in 2015 after working as a business coach for over a decade. She saw what TWCC could be and used her skills and passion for the collective to push it to where it is today.
Over the last three decades The Women’s Coaching Collective have helped thousands of women open and expand businesses across hundreds of industries, making a significant impact on local communities and helping create and support business leaders.
Our ethics and values are the utmost importance to us. At TWCC we believe in:
Collaboration, Sustainability, Respect, Intersectionality, and Integrity.
